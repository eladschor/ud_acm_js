What is DelHack?

DelHack is UD's premier student run hackathon, a 24 hour [caffeine-fueled] programming event, founded by developers and created for developers. It's not an event meant for breaking into systems or hacking into software - it's about putting something together and scrapping an idea with minimal resources in an incredibly small amount of time.

We're a community of builders. DelHack will be a place for students to strengthen their technical abilities, develop or discover new ones, and work on technical projects relevant to their own interests - whether it be hardware or software. It's a place that's meant to turn an idea into a reality, all within 24 hours.

FAQ

Who can attend?
This event is open to any UD enrolled student and is free to enroll in. We encourage students from every year and any major to attend.

What should I bring?
Bring your laptop and charger for the event. If you're working on a hardware hack, you'll need to bring your own supplies. We provide the power and wifi.

I don't know programming.
No worries. That's the point of this event - to come and learn, to immerse yourself in a new environment, and to teach yourself something you wouldn't have otherwise.

What if I don't have an idea?
Not a problem. We'll have mentors and student volunteers walking around and they can help you thing of something to hack on, or you can even join another team.

How many people on a team?
Teams can range from 1-4 in size.

What if I don't know anyone?
That's completely fine. The point is to come and learn something new and work with new people. You can go ahead and form a team on the spot.
